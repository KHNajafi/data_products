A Preamble .. Data Science for Humanity

Modern transit, advanced economies, diversity, world class education and services are all part of modern city life around the developed world (like in my home city of Toronto). However homelessness and marginalization are also real features, and often we don't know much about it except for maybe some brief observations during our daily lives to work, school, and around. Does homelessness affect only a small portion of a very specific group, like only adult men? What are the services that are provided to groups affected? Where are these services offered?

This app is modern visualization applied to something outside of business or tech, and represents data science's potential to positively impact humanity at large; especially those who are not typically beneficiaries of cutting edge technology

"To meet increasing demand for shelter, the City has been adding beds to...over 2,800 more beds than were available November 1, 2016" (City of Toronto Report)

Under-the-Hood - About the App..

The app is built on a dataset from the City of Toronto's Open Data Program online. Though the outputs are elementary, it lays a foundation for using data science to help with a solution to homelessness in Toronto and hopefully for cities around the world.

The app is of course created in R using shiny, with leaflet, ggplot2 and directlabels for the main visualizations, as well as data.table, lubridate, and dplyr.
